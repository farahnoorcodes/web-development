Nav, Main, Section, Span tags

Nav:

        <nav> — Block-level, semantic

Wraps a group of navigation links (menu, sidebar links, pagination).
Not every link needs to be inside <nav> — only major navigation blocks.
Example: your site's top menu (Home, About, Contact).


    Home
    About
    Services
    Contact

Main:

        <main> — Block-level, semantic

Wraps the primary content of the page (the unique stuff, not header/footer/nav).
Only ONE <main> per page.
Helps screen readers jump straight to the real content.
Here's a focused example of just <main> in isolation:


    
Welcome to My Website

    
This is the primary content of the page — the actual reason someone visited.



A more complete example, showing where <main> fits alongside other tags:
    

    Home
    About





    
About Me

    
I am a Computer Engineering student learning HTML step by step.


    
My Goals

    
I want to build strong skills in both software and hardware.

section:

<section> — a semantic, block-level HTML tag used to group related content together under one topic, usually with its own heading. 
Example: a "Services" section, a "Testimonials" section
Different from <div> — <div> has no meaning, <section> says "this is a distinct chunk of content".
Here's a focused example of just <section> in isolation:


    
My Skills

    
I am learning HTML and I already know Python and C programming.



Key rules for <section>:

Groups content that belongs together under one topic
Should usually have its own heading (h2, h3, etc.) so it's clear what the section is about
You can have many sections on one page (unlike <main>, which only appears once)
A more complete example, showing multiple sections on one page:



    

        
About Me

        
I am a Computer Engineering student at BZU, currently in my third semester.

    


    

        
My Skills

        
I know Python, C, and I am currently learning HTML step by step.

    


    

        
My Goals

        
I want to build strong projects that combine both software and hardware.

span:

    <span>- Inline, NOT semantic

Used to style or target a small piece of text inside a line, without breaking the flow
Opposite of <div> in behavior: <div> is block-level, <span> is inline
Example: making just one word bold/colored inside a sentence, without starting a new line.
Here's a focused example of just <span> in isolation:

My favorite subject is Computer Engineering and I enjoy coding.
Comparing <span> to <div>:
This starts on its own line

This is a sentence with a highlighted word inside it.

Another common use case — marking specific words for later styling:

Farah is learning HTML, Python, and C.
